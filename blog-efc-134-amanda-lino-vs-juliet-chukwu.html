EFC 134: Amanda ‘Maddog’ Lino Defends Her Crown Against Juliet Chukwu

Date

Challenger

Title
EFC Featherweight World Title

The Biggest Fight in Women’s African MMA

Amanda “Maddog” Lino stepped into the cage at EFC 134 carrying the weight of a championship belt and the expectations of an entire continent. On the other side stood Juliet Chukwu — undefeated, technically polished, and with the momentum of a fighter who believes every title fight is hers to take. What followed was a masterclass in championship-level mixed martial arts, delivered by a woman who has built her legacy one hard fight at a time.

This was not the first time Amanda had defended her EFC Featherweight World Title, and if the performance at EFC 134 is any measure, it will not be the last. But Chukwu brought challenges that no previous opponent had presented in quite the same way — a relentless forward pressure, sharp counter-striking, and a grappling game that had finished fighters before they had time to think.

How the Fight Unfolded

Round One: Setting the Tempo

Amanda opened with the composed, measured approach that has defined her championship reign. She controlled distance with her jab, read Chukwu’s forward pressure, and began establishing the clinch entries that would become central to her game plan as the fight progressed. Chukwu landed cleanly in the early exchanges, testing whether the champion would wilt under early aggression. She did not.

Round Two: Taking Control

The second round was where the fight’s trajectory became clear. Amanda began landing her combinations with greater accuracy, mixing levels to keep Chukwu uncertain about where the next attack was coming from. Her takedown defence held firm against Chukwu’s attempts to change the venue of the fight, and her own offensive grappling began to impose itself — a sequence of takedowns and positional control that demonstrated the full breadth of a BJJ brown belt operating at championship pace.

Finishing Rounds: Championship Heart

The championship rounds revealed exactly what separates world champions from challengers. Where other fighters have faded under the accumulation of five rounds of elite-level competition, Amanda found another gear. Her movement remained sharp, her combinations remained meaningful, and her cage IQ — the ability to read the fight, adjust, and execute under fatigue — was the decisive factor. Chukwu pushed until the final horn, but the fight was Amanda’s.

Two world championships. One standard. The belt stays in Ballito.

What This Win Means

A successful title defence at EFC 134 cements Amanda Lino’s status as the most decorated active women’s fighter in African MMA. She is the only fighter in EFC history to hold the Featherweight World Title multiple times. Her resume — IMMAF Featherweight World Champion 2014, European NAGA Women’s Absolute Champion, BJJ Brown Belt — reads like a document of sustained excellence across more than a decade of elite competition.

What makes this trajectory remarkable is not just the results, but what they are built on. Amanda’s preparation for EFC 134 was managed entirely from Maddog Performance Institute in Ballito — the facility she founded specifically to provide the training infrastructure that elite performance demands. Every session, every recovery protocol, every tactical adjustment in the eight weeks before fight night happened at the gym she built for exactly this purpose.

Amanda on the Win

Every camp teaches you something new. Juliet is an incredible fighter and she pushed me in ways I had to prepare for meticulously. But that is what fight camp at Maddog is designed for — to make sure that nothing that happens inside the cage on fight night catches you off guard. We prepared for everything. And we delivered.
