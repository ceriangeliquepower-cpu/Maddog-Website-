"I coach with the same relentless standard I hold myself to in competition. The expectation is excellence — and I know what it takes to get there."

Amanda “Maddog” Lino is one of the most decorated female combat sports athletes South Africa has ever produced. A 2× EFC World Champion, IMMAF Featherweight World Champion, Africa’s first female double-division champion, and BJJ Brown Belt — she began her MMA career simply to get fit, and went on to change the sport on the continent. She founded Maddog Performance Institute to bring world-champion level coaching and recovery to the KZN North Coast.

Her coaching philosophy is shaped by everything that made her a champion: relentless preparation, technical mastery, mental fortitude, and the conviction that recovery and nutrition are as important as the session itself. She founded Maddog Performance Institute to build the performance environment she wished had existed when she was coming up — and to give the KZN North Coast access to world-class coaching it had never had before.

Amanda holds coaching certifications across MMA, striking, and grappling disciplines. Her fight camp preparation methodology is informed by 15+ years of training under some of Africa’s most respected coaches, refined through two world title campaigns at the highest level of African MMA. She is also a vocal advocate for women in sport and youth empowerment through martial arts — running subsidised self-defence programmes across the North Coast in partnership with local schools.

“Jiu Jitsu offers essential everyday skills and profound lifestyle benefits — for students of all ages.”

York Lawrence is the Head Coach of Dolphin Coast Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, based at Maddog Performance Institute. With a lifelong foundation in martial arts, York transitioned from Karate and Kickboxing to the “gentle art” in 2005, beginning his journey at Novagen BJJ — one of South Africa’s pioneering academies.

In 2023, York reached a major milestone by receiving his Black Belt from multiple-time world champion Penny Thomas. His transition into coaching began during the COVID-19 pandemic, where a dedicated small group of students evolved into what is now Dolphin Coast BJJ. Driven by the belief that Jiu Jitsu offers essential everyday skills and profound lifestyle benefits, York expanded his reach in 2025 by launching an engaging Kids Program — fostering a community where students of all ages can experience the discipline, resilience, and technical depth of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu.

“My coaching blends medical insight, disciplined training, and performance-driven strategy — giving every client the confidence to train intelligently, safely, and effectively.”

Wren Kobus is Maddog’s in-house Registered Nurse and Strength & Conditioning Coach — a rare combination of clinical expertise and elite competitive experience. A Proteas-selected Master 1 athlete and current KZNPLF Master 1 record holder, she competes at provincial and national level while simultaneously coaching, directing, and practising. Her dual role as a medical professional and performance coach makes her one of the most uniquely qualified practitioners on the KZN North Coast.

As Director of the KwaZulu-Natal Powerlifting Association and a certified Coach and Referee under the South African Powerlifting Federation, Wren brings institutional knowledge and elite competitive experience to every strength session she leads. Her clinical background as a Registered Nurse informs every training prescription — she understands the body not just as an athlete, but as a medical professional.

A mother of three, Wren brings a deep understanding of the female body, real-world demands, and the mindset required to build strength around life’s responsibilities. Her coaching approach — available in-person at Maddog and through online and hybrid programmes — combines medical precision with performance strategy to build strong bodies, resilient minds, and results that last.
